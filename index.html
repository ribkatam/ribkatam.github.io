About

I am currently an MS candidate in Electrical Engineering at The George Washington University, building on a rigorous Computer Science foundation from KAIST[cite: 5, 9].

My academic journey is defined by a deliberate transition from algorithmic intelligence to physical systems. While my background in CS provided a strong foundation in modern machine learning, I sought to understand the mathematical foundations—linear systems and stochastic processes—underlying neural dynamics[cite: 75, 81].

My goal is to reverse-engineer the principles of sparsity and stability found in the brain to solve the efficiency disparities in current AI models[cite: 86, 69].
